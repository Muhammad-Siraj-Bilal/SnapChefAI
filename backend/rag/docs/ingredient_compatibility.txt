INGREDIENT COMPATIBILITY & FLAVOUR PAIRING SCIENCE
===================================================

This guide covers the science of why ingredients pair well, classic combinations, and how to balance the four core taste dimensions in any dish.

== THE FOUR TASTE AXES ==

1. SALT: Sodium enhances flavour perception, suppresses bitterness, and bridges other flavours.
   - Sources: Salt, soy sauce, fish sauce, miso, anchovies, capers, Parmesan, olives, tamari

2. ACID: Brightens, cuts richness, enhances freshness, and makes other flavours more vivid.
   - Sources: Lemon juice, lime juice, vinegar (balsamic, rice, apple cider, red wine), tamarind, sumac, verjuice, white wine, tomatoes, yogurt, buttermilk

3. FAT: Carries flavour, adds richness, rounds out sharpness, and enables Maillard browning.
   - Sources: Olive oil, butter, ghee, sesame oil, coconut oil, avocado, nuts, cream, tahini

4. HEAT/SPICE: Adds perceived warmth, complexity, and depth.
   - Sources: Chili, black pepper, ginger, horseradish, wasabi, Sichuan pepper, white pepper, mustard

The Rule of Balance: If a dish tastes flat → add acid or salt. If it tastes harsh/sour → add fat. If it tastes too rich → add acid. If it tastes dull → add spice.

Chocolate (dark 70%+):

Garlic:
+ Almost everything savoury. Pairs best with: olive oil, onion, ginger, lemon, chili, rosemary, thyme, tomato, soy sauce.

== UMAMI BOOSTERS ==

Umami (glutamate + inosinate/guanylate synergy) creates depth and savoury richness.
Combine two sources for exponential effect:

High-glutamate foods: Tomatoes (especially cooked/dried), Parmesan, miso, soy sauce, nutritional yeast, seaweed, fish sauce, anchovies, mushrooms (especially dried shiitake), Worcestershire sauce.

High-inosinate foods: Meat, chicken, fish, katsuobushi (dried bonito).

Synergy examples:
- Tomato + Parmesan (pasta): Glutamate × 2 = intense umami
- Dashi (kombu + bonito): Glutamate + inosinate synergy = 7–8× umami vs each alone
- Miso + dried shiitake: Glutamate + guanylate synergy
- Soy sauce + fish sauce: Glutamate stacking; use lesser amounts of each
- Anchovy + tomato paste (bolognese): Classic Italian umami depth trick

Add a small amount of these to any savoury dish to deepen flavour without obvious taste:
- ½ tsp miso dissolved into soup or stew
- 1–2 anchovy fillets melted in olive oil (invisible but deeply savoury)
- 1 tbsp soy sauce added to beef stew
- A Parmesan rind simmered in soup (remove before serving)
- 1 tsp fish sauce in guacamole or salsa

== FAT-SOLUBLE FLAVOUR CARRIERS ==

Many aromatic compounds are fat-soluble, meaning fat carries and amplifies their flavour.

Always bloom spices in fat (oil, butter, ghee) for 30–60 seconds before adding liquids:
- Cumin seeds in heated oil → nutty, intense
- Mustard seeds in heated oil → pop and become milder, nuttier
- Turmeric in fat → colour and flavour bloom (also increases bioavailability)
- Garlic in butter → sweeter, more complex than boiling in water
- Chili flakes in olive oil (aglio e olio) → transfers capsaicin into oil

== ACID PAIRING BY DISH TYPE ==

Dish type               | Best acid           | Amount guide
Rich braised beef       | Red wine / balsamic | ½ cup per 1.5kg at cooking start
Creamy pasta           | Lemon juice         | 1–2 tbsp at finish (off heat)
Fish                   | Lemon / white wine  | Squeeze at finish preserves brightness
Indian curry           | Tamarind / tomato   | Stir in at cook; 2 tbsp tamarind paste
Asian stir-fry         | Rice vinegar        | 1 tsp at end; do not cook long
Guacamole/salsa        | Lime juice          | 1–2 tbsp; generous and fresh
Salad dressing         | Ratio: 3 parts oil : 1 part acid (classic vinaigrette)
Soups (finishing)      | Apple cider vinegar | ½ tsp per litre; brightens without taste

== WINE + FOOD PAIRING BASICS ==

Light white wines (Pinot Grigio, Sauvignon Blanc): Fish, shellfish, salads, goat cheese
Full white (Chardonnay): Lobster, cream sauces, roast chicken, buttery fish
Rosé: Charcuterie, light pasta, grilled vegetables, salmon
Light red (Pinot Noir): Salmon, duck, mushrooms, grilled chicken
Medium red (Merlot, Sangiovese): Pasta bolognese, pizza, lamb, tomato-based stews
Full red (Cabernet Sauvignon, Shiraz): Beef steak, lamb, aged cheese, chocolate desserts

Rule: Match weight to weight. Light dish = light wine. Acid in wine = acid in food.
