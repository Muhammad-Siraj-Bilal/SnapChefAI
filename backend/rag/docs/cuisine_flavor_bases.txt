CUISINE FLAVOR PROFILES & SIGNATURE INGREDIENTS
================================================

A guide to the foundational flavor bases, essential spices, and cooking techniques that define each cuisine's identity.

== INDIAN CUISINE ==
Flavor profile: Warm, complex, aromatic, often spiced

Aromatics base (for most dishes): onion + garlic + ginger (finely minced or pasted, sautéed in ghee or oil)

Essential spices:
- Garam masala (warming blend of cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, black pepper, cumin, coriander)
- Turmeric: golden colour, earthy, anti-inflammatory
- Cumin (seeds and powder): nutty, earthy
- Coriander powder: lemony, floral
- Chili (Kashmiri for colour; cayenne for heat)
- Fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi): bitter, maple-like finish
- Mustard seeds: pungent, nutty when tempered in oil
- Curry leaves: citrusy, fragrant

Acids/balance: Tomatoes, tamarind, yogurt, lemon
Fats: Ghee (clarified butter) for richness; mustard oil in Bengali cooking; coconut oil in South India
Key techniques: Blooming spices in fat (tadka/tarka), slow caramelisation of onions (30–45 min for depth)

Regional notes: North Indian = dairy-heavy (cream, paneer). South Indian = coconut + tamarind. Bengali = mustard + poppy seeds. Rajasthani = chili-forward.

== ITALIAN CUISINE ==
Flavor profile: Fresh, bright, ingredient-led, restrained

Aromatics base (soffritto): onion + carrot + celery, gently softened in olive oil (never browned for delicate sauces)

Essential ingredients:
- San Marzano tomatoes: sweeter, less acidic than standard
- Extra-virgin olive oil: finish dishes with a drizzle; do not overheat
- Parmigiano-Reggiano or Pecorino Romano: salty umami
- Fresh basil: add off heat to preserve volatiles
- Garlic: sliced thin for fragrant infusion; never browned
- Capers + olives: briny, South Italian influence
- Anchovies: dissolve in oil for background umami (invisible but essential in puttanesca)

Acids/balance: Lemon zest, white wine, good-quality red wine vinegar
Key techniques: Al dente pasta (firm to bite), pasta water emulsification (adds starch for silky sauce), risotto mantecatura (stir in cold butter off heat)

Regional notes: Northern = butter-based, cream, risotto. Central = tomato, pork. Southern = olive oil, seafood, chili.

== JAPANESE CUISINE ==
Flavor profile: Umami-forward, clean, precise, balanced

The five tastes in Japanese cooking: umami (dashi), saltiness (soy/miso), sweetness (mirin), acidity (rice vinegar), bitter (matcha, burdock)

Dashi (base stock): kombu + katsuobushi (dried bonito flakes). Vegan dashi: kombu only or kombu + shiitake.

Essential ingredients:
- Soy sauce (shoyu): light (usukuchi) for delicate dishes; dark (koikuchi) for richer
- Mirin: sweet rice wine; adds gloss and gentle sweetness
- Sake: cooking wine; tenderises, removes odours
- Miso: fermented soybean paste (white = mild, shiro; red = intense, aka)
- Rice vinegar: mild, clean acidity for sushi rice, pickles, dressings
- Sesame oil (toasted): finishing drizzle only
- Wasabi, shichimi togarashi: heat elements

Key techniques: Yakimono (grilling with tare glaze), nimono (simmered in dashi), agemono (deep frying for tempura — ice-cold batter, minimal mixing, high heat), teriyaki (marinate then glaze on high heat)

== KOREAN CUISINE ==
Flavor profile: Spicy, fermented, sweet-salty, bold

Essential ingredients:
- Gochujang: fermented red chili paste (sweet, spicy, deeply complex)
- Gochugaru: Korean red chili flakes (less heat than gochujang, vivid colour)
- Doenjang: fermented soybean paste (earthier, more pungent than Japanese miso)
- Soy sauce (ganjang): lighter; used as salt equivalent
- Sesame oil (toasted): essential finishing oil
- Garlic and ginger: heavyweight aromatics in most dishes
- Scallions: used fresh (as garnish) and cooked (in stews, pancakes)
- Kimchi: fermented cabbage; side dish and cooking ingredient

Key techniques: Bulgogi marinade (pear or kiwi enzyme tenderiser), grill/griddle at table (ssam), jjigae (deeply flavoured stews), pancakes (jeon), bibimbap (mixed rice bowl with gochujang binding)

== MEXICAN CUISINE ==
Flavor profile: Earthy, smoky, acidic, layered heat

Three chili heat levels: Mild (poblano, ancho), Medium (guajillo, pasilla), Hot (chipotle, arbol, habanero)

Essential ingredients:
- Dried chilies: ancho (fruity), guajillo (tangy), chipotle in adobo (smoky), mulato (chocolate)
- Cumin: essential in almost every savoury dish
- Mexican oregano: more citrusy than Mediterranean; used in stews and marinades
- Epazote: pungent herb used in black beans and soups
- Tomatillos: tart green base for salsa verde
- Cilantro (coriander leaf): abundant garnish for tacos, guacamole
- Lime: finishing acid on almost everything
- Lard: traditional fat for refried beans and tamale masa (can sub vegetable shortening)

Key techniques: Charring tomatillos/garlic/onion directly on dry comal for smokiness, dry-toasting dried chilies (30 seconds each side, then rehydrate in hot water), mole (complex chili-chocolate-spice sauce requiring hours)

== THAI CUISINE ==
Flavor profile: Balance of hot, sour, sweet, salty in every dish

Flavor balance principle: Every Thai dish targets all four tastes simultaneously.

Essential ingredients:
- Fish sauce (nam pla): primary salt source; essential depth
- Palm sugar or coconut sugar: primary sweet source (not white sugar)
- Lime juice: primary acid (not vinegar)
- Bird's eye chilies: primary heat (can sub Thai chilies)
- Lemongrass: citrusy and floral; bruise before use
- Galangal (not ginger): distinctive pine/citrus flavour for Tom Yum, curries
- Kaffir lime leaves (makrut): aromatic, citrusy; tear to release aroma
- Shrimp paste (kapi): fermented base for curry pastes

Curry paste bases: Red = dried red chilies + shrimp paste + lemongrass + galangal + kaffir lime. Green = fresh green chilies + basil + same aromatics.

Key techniques: High-heat wok (pad = stir-fry), coconut milk curry (crack coconut cream before adding paste), som tam (mortar pestle bruising)

== MIDDLE EASTERN / ARABIC CUISINE ==
Flavor profile: Warm spices, herbs, citrus, sesame, legumes

Essential spice blends:
- Za'atar: thyme + sesame + sumac + oregano (bright, herby)
- Baharat (7 spice): allspice + black pepper + cinnamon + coriander + cumin + cloves + nutmeg
- Ras el hanout (Morocco): complex blend up to 30 spices
- Dukkah (Egypt): roasted nuts + sesame + coriander + cumin

Essential ingredients:
- Tahini: sesame paste; base for hummus, baba ganoush, sauces
- Sumac: tart, lemony burgundy-coloured spice; replaces lemon when you want colour
- Pomegranate molasses: sweet-sour; used in marinades and dressings
- Rose water / orange blossom water: floral note in desserts (baklava, rice pudding)
- Preserved lemons: salty, intensely citrusy; use rind only in tagines and dips
- Chickpeas: hummus, falafel, stews
- Lamb: dominant protein; braised with warm spices

Key techniques: Slow braise with spice (tagine), flatbread baking (sajj), meze (small plates), charcoal grilling (mashawi), layered rice (maqlouba)

== FRENCH CUISINE ==
Flavor profile: Rich, buttery, technical, wine-forward

The five mother sauces:
1. Béchamel: butter + flour roux + milk. Base for mac & cheese, lasagna, croque monsieur.
2. Velouté: roux + white stock (chicken/fish). Base for supremes and vol-au-vents.
3. Espagnole (brown sauce): roux + brown beef stock + tomato. Base for demi-glace.
4. Hollandaise: emulsified egg yolk + clarified butter + lemon. For eggs Benedict.
5. Tomat: tomatoes + aromatics + stock. Base for many braises.

Aromatics: Mirepoix = onion + carrot + celery (2:1:1 ratio). Bouquet garni = thyme + bay + parsley stems tied.

Key techniques: Deglazing with wine or cognac, reduction sauces (reducing by half to concentrate), beurre blanc (butter sauce), confit (cook in fat at low temp)

== CHINESE CUISINE ==
Flavor profile: Umami, balance of five flavours (sour-sweet-salty-bitter-savory)

Regional flavour bases:
- Cantonese: Light, delicate, fresh; oyster sauce, ginger, garlic, soy
- Sichuan: Numbing spice (ma la); Sichuan peppercorn + doubanjiang (fermented chili bean paste)
- Shanghainese: Sweet-soy (red braise/hongshao); dark soy + sugar + Shaoxing wine
- Hunan: Dry heat (hot without numbing); dried chilies, garlic, vinegar

Essential ingredients:
- Shaoxing rice wine: cooking wine; removes odours, adds depth
- Dark soy sauce: colour and sweetness (use sparingly)
- Light soy sauce: everyday salt and flavour
- Oyster sauce: thick, sweet-savoury; finish stir-fries
- Doubanjiang: Sichuan chili bean paste; pungent, spicy
- Five spice powder: star anise + cloves + cinnamon + Sichuan pepper + fennel

Key techniques: Velveting (marinate protein in cornstarch + egg white + soda; blanch before stir-fry), wok hei (charred smoky flavour from high-heat tossing), clay pot braises

== GREEK CUISINE ==
Flavor profile: Bright, herby, lemony, olive-forward

Essential ingredients:
- Olive oil: Extra-virgin as finishing; use generously
- Lemon: Lemon juice in almost every savoury dish
- Oregano: Dried more than fresh; stronger than Italian
- Garlic: Used raw and cooked
- Feta cheese: Salty, tangy; block in brine is superior to crumbled
- Tomatoes: Sun-ripened; essential in salads, stews
- Lamb: Primary protein in traditional cooking

Key techniques: Souvlaki marinade (lemon + olive oil + oregano + garlic), slow-roasted lamb (kleftiko — sealed in parchment), spanakopita (feta + spinach in phyllo), tzatziki (strained yogurt + cucumber + garlic + dill)

== MOROCCAN CUISINE ==
Flavor profile: Warm spice, sweet-savoury, dried fruit contrast

Essential ingredients:
- Preserved lemons: Fermented salt lemons; rind only used
- Ras el hanout: 10–30 spice blend; essential in tagines
- Harissa: Hot smoked chili paste; condiment and cooking base
- Saffron: golden colour, floral; essential in couscous and bastilla
- Orange blossom water: floral; in mint tea and desserts
- Dates + prunes + apricots: Sweet contrast in savoury lamb/chicken tagines

Key techniques: Low-and-slow tagine braising (the conical lid circulates steam), couscous steaming over stew (not just boiling in water), bastilla layers of phyllo with pigeon/chicken + egg + almonds + cinnamon sugar
