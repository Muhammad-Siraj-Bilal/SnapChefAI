FOOD SAFETY: TEMPERATURES, STORAGE & CROSS-CONTAMINATION
=========================================================

Essential food safety knowledge to prevent foodborne illness and ensure safe cooking practices.

== SAFE MINIMUM INTERNAL COOKING TEMPERATURES ==

Always use a food thermometer inserted into the thickest part of the food (away from bone, fat, gristle).

Meat & Poultry:
Food                              | Safe Temp (°C) | Safe Temp (°F) | Rest Time
Whole poultry (chicken, turkey)   | 74             | 165            | 3 minutes
Poultry pieces (breast, thigh)    | 74             | 165            | 3 minutes
Ground poultry (burgers, meatball)| 74             | 165            | None needed
Ground beef/pork/lamb             | 71             | 160            | None needed
Beef steaks (whole cut)           | 63             | 145            | 3 minutes
Pork (whole cuts, chops, ribs)    | 63             | 145            | 3 minutes
Veal                              | 63             | 145            | 3 minutes
Lamb (whole cuts)                 | 63             | 145            | 3 minutes
Lamb (ground)                     | 71             | 160            | None needed
Ham (raw, fresh)                  | 63             | 145            | 3 minutes
Ham (pre-cooked, reheating)       | 60             | 140            | None needed
Rabbit                            | 74             | 165            | 3 minutes
Venison/game                      | 74             | 165            | 3 minutes

Seafood:
Food                              | Safe Temp (°C) | Safe Temp (°F) | Notes
Fish (all types)                  | 63             | 145            | Flakes easily, opaque
Shrimp, lobster, crab             | 74             | 165            | Shell turns pink, flesh opaque
Clams, oysters, mussels           | 74             | 165            | Shell opens; discard closed ones
Scallops                          | 63             | 145            | Milky white, firm
Squid, octopus                    | 74             | 165            | Firm and white

Eggs & Egg Dishes:
Food                              | Safe Temp (°C) | Safe Temp (°F)
Eggs (fried, poached, scrambled)  | 71             | 160 (entire egg solid)
Egg dishes (quiche, frittata)     | 71             | 160
Casseroles with eggs              | 74             | 165

Reheating & Leftovers:
- All leftovers and pre-cooked foods: Reheat to 74°C (165°F) internal temperature
- Soups, sauces, gravies: Bring to a rolling boil
- Do not reheat food more than once

Danger Zone: 4°C to 60°C (40°F to 140°F)
Bacteria multiply rapidly in this range. Never leave perishable food in this zone for more than 2 hours (1 hour if ambient temp is above 32°C/90°F).

== CROSS-CONTAMINATION PREVENTION ==

Colour-coded cutting board system:
- Red: Raw meat (beef, pork, lamb)
- Yellow: Raw poultry (chicken, turkey, duck)
- Blue: Raw seafood
- Green: Produce (vegetables and fruit)
- White: Dairy and bakery
- Brown/Tan: Cooked food and ready-to-eat

Hand washing:
- Wash hands with soap for minimum 20 seconds before and after handling raw protein
- Wash after touching eyes, nose, mouth, or phone during cooking
- Sanitise sink, counters, and handles after working with raw poultry

Refrigerator organisation (top to bottom, by required cooking temperature):
1. Top shelves: Ready-to-eat foods, leftovers, cooked food (labelled with date)
2. Middle shelves: Dairy, eggs
3. Lower shelves: Raw seafood
4. Bottom drawer: Raw poultry (lowest shelf = lowest contamination risk if drips)
5. Separate drawer if available: Raw beef/pork/lamb

Thawing safely (never on counter at room temperature):
- Best method: In fridge overnight (allows slow, safe thaw)
- Quick method: Submerged in cold water (change water every 30 min); cook immediately after
- Microwave method: Defrost setting; cook immediately after — some areas partially cook
- Never refreeze thawed raw protein without cooking it first

Marinating rules:
- Always marinate in the refrigerator, not on the counter
- Never reuse marinade as a sauce without boiling it first for 3 minutes
- Use separate utensils for raw and cooked meat in the same session

== HIGH-RISK FOODS (Extra caution required) ==

- Raw or undercooked eggs: Risk of Salmonella. Use pasteurised eggs for raw preparations (hollandaise, Caesar dressing, tiramisu, mousse).
- Raw or undercooked poultry: Salmonella and Campylobacter. Always reach 74°C internal.
- Raw shellfish: Vibrio bacteria. High risk for immunocompromised individuals.
- Raw or undercooked pork: Yersinia and Trichinella (much rarer now but still present). Minimum 63°C.
- Raw sprouts (bean sprouts, alfalfa): Warm moist environment ideal for E. coli/Salmonella. Cook before consuming if vulnerable.
- Unpasteurised milk and soft cheeses: Listeria risk; avoid during pregnancy.
- Rice (cooked, stored): Bacillus cereus survives cooking and toxin forms at room temperature. Cool within 1 hour; refrigerate and use within 3–5 days. Reheat thoroughly.
- Cut melons: Bacteria colonise cut surface quickly. Refrigerate immediately; use within 3 days.

== SAFE FOOD COOLING ==

Large batches of hot food must be cooled quickly to avoid bacterial growth:
- Cool from 60°C to 20°C within 2 hours
- Cool from 20°C to 4°C within 4 hours (total: 6 hours maximum)

Methods to speed cooling:
- Divide into smaller shallow containers (increases surface area)
- Place pot in ice bath in sink; stir every 15 minutes
- Stir with a chilled metal spoon or cooling paddle
- Place in blast chiller if available

Labelling: Always label refrigerated/frozen leftovers with: Content + Date stored
