ALLERGEN SUBSTITUTION GUIDE FOR COOKING
========================================

This guide covers safe, tested substitutions for the eight major allergens and common dietary restrictions. All ratios are for direct replacement in standard recipes.

== PEANUTS & TREE NUTS ==
Peanut butter substitutes:
- Sunflower seed butter (sunbutter): 1:1 ratio. Slightly less sweet; add ½ tsp vanilla to compensate.
- Soy nut butter: 1:1 ratio. Closest in protein and fat profile.
- Tahini (sesame paste): 1:1 in savoury dishes. In sweet recipes, use ½ ratio and add sweetener.
- Pumpkin seed butter: 1:1. Green colour may affect appearance of baked goods.

Chopped peanut/nut substitutes:
- Roasted sunflower seeds: 1:1 by volume. Toast first for best flavour.
- Toasted pumpkin seeds (pepitas): 1:1. Great in salads and stir-fries.
- Roasted chickpeas (crumbled): 1:1. Adds crunch to salads and baked goods.
- Hemp hearts: 1:1 in smoothies, granola, baked goods.
- Crispy fried onions: For savoury crunch topping.

Hidden peanut names to avoid: Arachis oil, groundnuts, monkey nuts, beer nuts, mixed nuts, marzipan (may contain).

Cross-contamination risk: Peanuts are processed in same facilities as tree nuts. Always check labels: "May contain traces of peanuts."

== DAIRY (Milk, Butter, Cream, Cheese) ==

Milk substitutes:
- Oat milk: 1:1. Best for baking; adds slight sweetness. Barista versions froth well.
- Soy milk: 1:1. Highest protein of plant milks. Works in savoury and sweet dishes.
- Almond milk: 1:1. Lower in protein; thinner consistency. Best in light sauces, smoothies.
- Coconut milk (full fat): 1:1 for richness in curries, soups, desserts.
- Cashew milk: 1:1. Creamy; good for cream-based sauces (not nut-free).
- Rice milk: 1:1. Thinnest consistency; best for drinks and light dishes only.

Butter substitutes:
- Vegan butter (Earth Balance, Violife): 1:1 for baking and cooking.
- Refined coconut oil: 1:1 (solid form). Neutral flavour when refined.
- Olive oil: ¾ cup per 1 cup butter in most baked goods. Reduces saturated fat.
- Applesauce: ½ cup per 1 cup butter in cakes/muffins. Reduces fat 30-40%.
- Mashed avocado: ½ cup per 1 cup butter. Works in brownies, chocolate cakes.

Heavy cream substitutes:
- Full-fat coconut cream (chilled): 1:1. Whips into peaks. Slight coconut flavour.
- Cashew cream: Blend 1 cup soaked cashews + ¾ cup water until smooth. 1:1. (Not nut-free.)
- Oat cream: 1:1 in soups, sauces. Does not whip.
- Silken tofu blended: 1:1 in creamy soups and dressings.

Cheese substitutes:
- Nutritional yeast: 3 tbsp = ¼ cup Parmesan-like flavour. Rich in B12.
- Vegan shredded cheese (Violife, Daiya): 1:1 in pizzas, quesadillas.
- Cashew cheese: Blend soaked cashews + lemon + garlic + nutritional yeast.
- Firm tofu (pressed, crumbled): Replaces cottage cheese or ricotta 1:1.

Hidden dairy names: Casein, whey, lactalbumin, lactoglobulin, lactulose, bovine, ghee, kefir.

== GLUTEN ==

All-purpose flour substitutes:
- Certified GF oat flour: 1:1 in most recipes. Adds slight oaty flavour.
- Rice flour (white): 1:1 in delicate baked goods.
- Almond flour: 1:1 by weight (not volume) — denser; add ¼ tsp xanthan gum per cup.
- Cassava flour: 1:1. Closest to wheat flour behaviour; no xanthan needed.
- Chickpea flour (besan): 1:1 in savoury applications (pakoras, socca, binding).
- GF all-purpose blend (Bob's Red Mill 1:1): 1:1. Already contains xanthan gum.

Breadcrumb substitutes:
- Crushed rice crackers: 1:1.
- Ground oats (GF certified): 1:1 for coating and stuffing.
- Crushed cornflakes (GF): 1:1 for crispy crusts.
- Almond meal: 1:1 in meatballs and patties.

Pasta substitutes:
- Rice noodles: 1:1. Cook 30% shorter time.
- Chickpea pasta: 1:1. Higher protein; cook per packet.
- Zucchini noodles (zoodles): Raw or lightly sautéed. No starch.
- Spaghetti squash: Roast and fork-separate into strands.

Soy sauce substitutes: Tamari (GF certified), coconut aminos (lower sodium).

Hidden gluten names: Wheat, barley, rye, spelt, kamut, triticale, malt, farro, durum, semolina, farina, bulgur, einkorn.

== EGGS ==

In baking (binding/moisture):
- Flax egg: 1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water, rest 5 min = 1 egg.
- Chia egg: 1 tbsp chia seeds + 3 tbsp water, rest 10 min = 1 egg.
- Aquafaba: 3 tbsp chickpea liquid = 1 egg (whipped = egg whites for meringue).
- Unsweetened applesauce: ¼ cup = 1 egg (moisture + mild binding).
- Mashed banana: ¼ cup = 1 egg (adds sweetness; use in banana bread, muffins).
- Commercial egg replacer (JUST Egg, Bob's): Per packet instructions.

As a binder in savoury cooking:
- Mashed potato or sweet potato: 2 tbsp per egg in patties and meatballs.
- Psyllium husk: 1 tsp + 3 tbsp water per egg. Strong binding.

For egg washes: Brush with plant milk or aquafaba for golden glaze.

== SHELLFISH ==

Direct protein substitutes:
- Hearts of palm (tinned): Shreds like crab; use in crab cakes 1:1.
- Jackfruit (young, tinned in brine): Shreds like lobster/crab. Drain and season.
- King oyster mushrooms (shredded): Mimics scallop texture when seared.
- Cauliflower steak: Substitute for large shellfish in grain bowls.

Umami/flavour substitutes (to replicate oceanic flavour):
- Dulse or nori seaweed: Chop and add to soups, stews.
- Kelp granules: ¼ tsp per serving adds oceanic depth.

== SOY ==

- Coconut aminos: 1:1 soy sauce substitute. Slightly sweeter; lower sodium.
- Sunflower seed butter: 1:1 for peanut-free AND soy-free recipes.
- Chickpea tofu (soy-free): Freeze chickpea flour block; press. Same uses as firm tofu.
- Pumpkin seed tofu: Similar method.

Hidden soy names: Miso, edamame, tamari, tempeh, textured vegetable protein (TVP), soya, shoyu, yuba.

== SESAME ==

- Hemp seeds: 1:1 as a topping.
- Poppy seeds: 1:1 as a topping or in dressings.
- Sunflower seed paste: 1:1 for tahini in hummus and dressings.
- Pumpkin seed butter: 1:1 for tahini in savoury applications.

Hidden sesame names: Gingelly, benne, til, gomasio, sesame oil, tahini.
