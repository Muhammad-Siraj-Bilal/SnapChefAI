HEALTHY INGREDIENT SWAPS & NUTRITIONAL UPGRADES
================================================

This guide provides quantified, tested substitutions to make recipes healthier without sacrificing flavour or texture.

== FAT REDUCTION SWAPS ==

Butter → Unsweetened Applesauce (in baked goods)
- Replace ½ to ¾ cup butter with ½ cup applesauce
- Reduces saturated fat by ~60%; adds moisture
- Best in muffins, quick breads, cakes
- Avoid in shortbread, croissants, pastries (structure depends on fat)

Butter → Greek Yogurt (in baked goods)
- Replace 1 cup butter with ½ cup full-fat Greek yogurt + 2 tbsp oil
- Adds protein; keeps moisture; slight tang
- Best in cakes, scones, pancakes

Butter → Mashed Avocado (in brownies/chocolate baked goods)
- Replace 1 cup butter with ½ cup mashed ripe avocado
- Monounsaturated fat replaces saturated fat
- Works best in dark/chocolate recipes (masks green colour)

Oil in dressings → Avocado/Tahini Base
- Replace ½ the oil with tahini or mashed avocado + citrus
- Reduces overall fat while increasing fibre and nutrients

Heavy cream → Silken Tofu (in blended soups)
- Replace 1 cup cream with 1 cup blended silken tofu
- Reduces fat ~70%; adds protein; nearly identical texture when blended

Heavy cream → Evaporated Skim Milk
- Replace 1 cup cream with 1 cup evaporated skim milk
- Reduces fat ~80%; slight sweetness; thicker than regular milk

Whole milk → 1% or Skim milk
- 1:1 direct swap in most recipes
- Baked goods: May lose slight richness. Add 1 tbsp olive oil per cup to compensate.

Mayonnaise → Greek Yogurt (in dressings/salads)
- Replace 1:1. Reduces fat ~75%; adds protein and probiotics
- Best in coleslaw, egg salad, pasta salad
- Avoid as a spread on hot items (yogurt can curdle)

Cream cheese → Ricotta + Greek Yogurt blend
- Replace ½ cream cheese with ½ ricotta + ¼ cup Greek yogurt
- Reduces fat ~40%; smoother texture; good in cheesecakes and dips

== SUGAR REDUCTION SWAPS ==

White sugar → Dates (in smoothies, energy balls, no-bake)
- Replace ¼ cup sugar with 3–4 Medjool dates (pitted, blended)
- Adds fibre, potassium, and natural sweetness
- Works in blended sauces, smoothies, energy balls

White sugar → Coconut Sugar
- 1:1 direct swap. Lower glycaemic index (~35 vs 65 for white)
- Adds slight caramel flavour; slightly darker baked goods
- Works in all baked goods

White sugar → Mashed Banana
- Replace ¼ cup sugar with ½ ripe mashed banana
- Reduces added sugar; adds potassium and fibre
- Best in muffins, pancakes, oatmeal bakes

White sugar → Stevia/Erythritol (for keto/low-carb)
- Erythritol: 1:1 by volume. Does not raise blood sugar. Slightly cooling taste.
- Stevia: Use only ¼ tsp per ¼ cup sugar (very concentrated)
- Best in cold preparations; some don't bake well at high heat

Maple syrup → Date syrup
- 1:1 swap. Lower glycaemic index; more caramel-like flavour

== CARBOHYDRATE / FLOUR SWAPS ==

White rice → Cauliflower rice
- Reduce carbs by ~80%. Steam or microwave cauliflower, pulse in food processor.
- Use 1:1 by volume in stir-fries, bowls, fried rice
- Does not expand like rice; adjust volume accordingly

White rice → Quinoa
- 1:1 cooked volume. Higher protein (8g/cup vs 5g for rice)
- Complete protein; cooks in 15 minutes vs rice's 18 minutes

White flour (all-purpose) → Almond Flour
- Replace 1 cup flour with 1 cup almond flour + ¼ tsp xanthan gum
- Higher fat, lower carbs; denser texture; gluten-free
- Best in cookies, pancakes, quick breads
- Not suitable for yeast bread without additional structure agents

White flour → Oat Flour
- Replace 1:1. Slightly nuttier, denser result
- Whole grain; more fibre than white flour
- Rolls less when used in pastry

White pasta → Chickpea pasta
- 1:1 cooked. ~25% more protein, ~20% more fibre than white pasta
- Cook 1–2 minutes less (firms quickly); do not overcook

White pasta → Lentil pasta / Black bean pasta
- 1:1 cooked. High in protein (15g+/serving) and fibre
- Slightly earthy flavour; pairs well with bold sauces

White bread → Whole grain sourdough (lower GI)
- GI of white bread: ~75. Sourdough wholegrain: ~50–54
- The fermentation pre-digests some starch

White potatoes → Sweet potatoes
- 1:1 in most recipes. Lower GI (~50 vs 80 for white potatoes)
- Higher in beta-carotene and vitamin A

== PROTEIN SWAPS ==

Ground beef → Lentils (in bolognese, tacos, chili)
- Replace 500g ground beef with 400g cooked lentils (or 175g dry)
- Reduces saturated fat ~90%; adds fibre; absorbs sauce well
- Add 1 tsp smoked paprika + 1 tsp cumin for meaty depth

Ground beef → Mushrooms + walnut mix
- Pulse 300g mushrooms + 150g walnuts; sauté until browned
- Intensely savoury; excellent umami. Replace 1:1 by volume

Chicken breast → Tofu (firm, pressed)
- Press tofu 30 min. Slice to same thickness. Marinate same as chicken.
- Cook slightly less (15–20 min at 200°C in oven vs 22–25 min for chicken)
- Protein: ~10g/100g (tofu) vs ~31g/100g (chicken). Supplement with edamame/lentils if needed.

Beef mince → Turkey mince
- 1:1. Reduces saturated fat ~60%. Less flavour; add Worcestershire or soy sauce.

Sausages → Chicken or turkey sausages
- Lower in saturated fat; check sodium content as often high

Eggs (protein source) → Chickpeas + nutritional yeast
- 3 tbsp chickpeas + 1 tbsp yeast provides similar protein to 1 egg
- Use in salads, grain bowls. Does not work for baking binding.

== SODIUM REDUCTION ==

Replace table salt with:
- Herbs and spices: Smoked paprika, cumin, garlic powder add perceived saltiness without sodium
- Lemon/lime zest and juice: Acid brightens flavour, reducing need for salt
- Vinegar (balsamic, apple cider, rice): Same perception-brightening effect
- Low-sodium soy sauce or tamari: ~40% less sodium
- Miso (small amounts): Concentrated umami; use sparingly

When scaling recipes: Salt does not scale linearly. When doubling a recipe, add 1.5× salt, then taste and adjust.

== FIBRE BOOSTERS (ADD TO ANY RECIPE) ==

- Chia seeds: 2 tbsp adds 8g fibre, 5g protein. Add to smoothies, porridge, overnight oats.
- Ground flaxseed: 2 tbsp adds 3.8g fibre, omega-3 fatty acids. Add to batters, meatballs.
- Psyllium husk: 1 tsp adds 3g fibre; thickens soups and smoothies.
- Hemp hearts: 3 tbsp adds 10g protein + omega-3. Works in almost any dish.
- Oat bran: Add 2 tbsp to porridge, pancake batter, smoothies.
- Lentils: Add ½ cup cooked lentils to soups, curries, stews invisibly.
